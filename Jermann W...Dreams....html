About the Wine

'Dreams' is Jermann's iconic barrel-fermented Chardonnay, first released in 1987. The name references the song 'Where Dreams Have No End' from U2's album The Joshua Tree. This wine represents the pinnacle of Jermann's white winemaking, with a small percentage of a "family secret" blend adding to its unique character.

"Winter was mild with above average temperatures, which allowed us to close pruning operations in mid-March. Flowering was earlier and was concomitant on almost all varieties and concentrated in a few days. Summer was dry with low rainfall. Our vineyards, that are located in very suitable areas, naturally limited the effects of drought and allowed the grapes to ripen optimally through harvest."

Vinification

Fermentation: Natural fermentation in stainless steel, no malolactic fermentation. Filtered and fined. Made with no animal byproducts (not certified in the US).

Tasting Notes

This Chardonnay shows exceptional complexity with notes of ripe stone fruits, citrus, and subtle oak influences from barrel aging. The absence of malolactic fermentation preserves the wine's fresh acidity, creating a perfect balance between richness and vibrancy.

Winery History

Jermann Winery was founded in 1881. Silvio Jermann is one of the most influential winemakers in Italy, turning his family's modest farm into a hallmark winery in Friuli. The winery is located at 45°59'19"N 13°28'09"E.

The 'Dreams' Legacy

First produced in 1987, 'Dreams' revolutionized Italian white winemaking by introducing techniques that emphasized both fruit purity and complexity through careful oak integration. It remains one of Italy's most celebrated and sought-after Chardonnays.

Organoleptic Profile

Tasting Notes

Visual

Brilliant golden yellow color with greenish reflections, indicating its excellent structure and aging potential.

Olfactory

Complex bouquet of ripe peach, apricot, and citrus fruits, with subtle notes of vanilla, toasted almonds, and delicate floral hints. The oak integration is impeccable, adding depth without overpowering the fruit.

Gustatory

Rich and creamy on the palate with a beautiful balance between ripe fruit flavors and refreshing acidity. Notes of baked apple, pear, and citrus are complemented by subtle oak spices. The finish is long and persistent, with mineral undertones that speak to its terroir.
