About the Wine

Jermann Tunina is the iconic wine that revolutionized Italian white winemaking. Created in 1975, it's a sophisticated blend that expresses the unique terroir of Friuli with exceptional complexity and aging potential.

Vintage Notes 2024

A favorable growing season with moderate temperatures and optimal rainfall distribution. The summer was warm without excessive heat spikes, allowing for perfect phenolic ripening while maintaining fresh acidity. Harvest occurred under ideal conditions.

Winery History

Jermann Winery was founded in 1881. Silvio Jermann is considered one of the most influential winemakers in Italy, transforming his family's estate into a benchmark for quality white wines. Tunina, named after a local historical figure, became legendary for its innovative approach to white blends.

Organoleptic Profile

Tasting Notes

Visual

Brilliant golden yellow color with greenish reflections, excellent consistency with slow, dense tears.

Olfactory

Complex and intense bouquet with notes of ripe tropical fruit (pineapple, mango), citrus zest, acacia flowers, and subtle hints of vanilla and toasted almonds from oak aging.

Gustatory

Full-bodied and rich on the palate with remarkable structure. Excellent balance between alcohol, acidity and softness. Long persistent finish with notes of honey, almond and mineral hints.

Pairing Suggestions

Ideal with elaborate fish dishes, shellfish, white meats and aged cheeses. Excellent as a meditation wine.

Aging Potential

Excellent evolution potential: 7-10 years, can develop further complexity with proper cellaring.
